# TrustOffice Video Library — Knowledge Base Guide

This knowledge base entry maps the Trustee 101 course curriculum to its video content, explaining what each module covers and how to navigate the course. The Trust Assistant uses this guide to recommend specific videos when users ask about trustee education topics.

---

## Course Overview

**Trustee 101** is a 27-lesson video course included with every TrustOffice subscription. It covers everything a new or accidental trustee needs to know — from understanding your fiduciary duties to building a repeatable administration system.

**Access:** Lessons 1–3 (Module 1) are free for all users. Lessons 4–27 (Modules 2–9) require an active TrustOffice subscription.

**Video hosting:** All videos are on Bunny Stream (Library 609821, Collection "Trustee 101 Course"). Embed URL format: `https://iframe.mediadelivery.net/embed/609821/{guid}`

## Module 1: The Trustee's Role (Lessons 1–3) — FREE

The foundational module that every trustee starts with. Covers what a trustee is, the legal duties you've taken on, and how to get organized in your first week. These three lessons serve as the free lead magnet — users who enroll get access to Module 1 immediately.

### Lesson 1: What Is a Trustee?
- **Video ID:** `b095719e-96c6-4a0a-a845-5f003777ff2f`
- **Duration:** 5:30
- **Key topics:** Definition of a trustee, how you became one (intentional vs. accidental), why the role matters more than most people realize
- **When to recommend:** User asks "what is a trustee?", "what did I sign up for?", "I was named trustee and don't know what that means"
- **Key insight:** Many trustees inherit the role unexpectedly through a family member's estate plan. This lesson validates that experience and sets expectations for the course.

### Lesson 2: Your Fiduciary Duties
- **Video ID:** `670222ba-cde6-4772-b3af-dac84fd91db0`
- **Duration:** 6:15
- **Key topics:** Three core fiduciary duties (loyalty, prudence, impartiality), the enforcement mechanism (beneficiaries can petition a court at any time), what "highest standard of care" actually means
- **When to recommend:** User asks about fiduciary duty, loyalty, prudence, impartiality, "what are my obligations?", "what happens if I make a mistake?"
- **Key insight:** The lesson emphasizes that the duty of loyalty is absolute — even when nobody got hurt. Self-dealing is prohibited regardless of outcome.

### Lesson 3: Meeting Minutes Are Not Optional
- **Video ID:** `9a645c14-7506-4b3d-81fa-ac17f17741cc`
- **Duration:** 8:01
- **Key topics:** UTC §703, Florida trustee $12,500 surcharge, 5-Minute Minute system, TrustOffice AI Minutes Builder, auto-stored/timestamped minutes
- **When to recommend:** User asks "do I need meeting minutes?", "how do I document trustee decisions?", "what if a beneficiary challenges me?", "how does TrustOffice help with minutes?"
- **Key insight:** This lesson includes a concrete day-by-day action checklist — not abstract advice but specific steps to take in the first week.

## How the Trust Assistant Uses This Guide

When a user asks a question that maps to a course topic, the Trust Assistant should:

1. **Identify the relevant lesson(s)** based on the key topics listed above
2. **Check access level:** Lessons 1–3 are free; Lessons 4–27 require an active subscription
3. **Provide a brief answer** from knowledge, then recommend the specific video for deeper understanding
4. **Include the embed URL** for free lessons: `https://iframe.mediadelivery.net/embed/609821/{video_id}`
5. **For subscription lessons,** let the user know they need an active TrustOffice subscription to access the full video

### Example Response Patterns

**Free lesson (1–3):**
> "That's covered in detail in Lesson 2: Your Fiduciary Duties. Here's the video: [embed link]. The three core duties are loyalty, prudence, and impartiality — and the key thing to understand is that beneficiaries can petition a court at any time to force you to account for your decisions."

**Subscription lesson (4–27):**
> "Great question — that's exactly what Lesson 5 covers in depth. Separating trust and personal finances is critical because commingling is the number one way trustees get into trouble. Upgrade to a TrustOffice subscription for access to the full 27-lesson course, including detailed guidance on trust banking and EIN requirements."
