# TrustOffice Guided Minutes

## What Are Guided Minutes?

Guided Minutes is TrustOffice's structured tool for documenting trust decisions. Instead of starting with a blank page and wondering what to write, Guided Minutes walks you through a series of prompts tailored to the type of decision you're making. It ensures you capture the right information, in the right format, every time.

The result is a clear, consistent record that strengthens your defensibility and keeps your trust administration on track.

## Why Use Guided Minutes?

Documenting trust decisions is one of the most important things a trustee can do — and one of the most commonly skipped. Without structure, it's easy to put off documentation or to write something incomplete. Guided Minutes removes the friction by making the process simple, guided, and thorough.

Benefits include:

- **Consistency:** Every type of decision follows the same format, making your records easy to review and compare over time
- **Completeness:** The prompts ensure you don't forget important details like your reasoning, alternatives considered, or beneficiary circumstances
- **Defensibility:** Well-structured minutes are your strongest defense if a decision is ever challenged
- **Time savings:** You don't have to figure out what to include — the system already knows

## What Types of Meetings It Covers

Guided Minutes offers templates for the most common trust administration decisions:

### Distribution Decisions
Document any distribution — approved, denied, or modified. The template prompts you to record the beneficiary, the request, the distribution standard that applies, your analysis, and your final decision with rationale.

### Investment Decisions
Record investment choices including asset purchases, sales, and rebalancing. Capture what you considered, what alternatives you evaluated, and how the decision aligns with your duty of prudence.

### Annual Reviews
Conduct a comprehensive review of the trust's status: investment performance, beneficiary circumstances, upcoming needs, and planned actions for the coming year.

### Professional Engagement
Document decisions to hire, work with, or change professional advisors such as attorneys, CPAs, or investment managers. Note the reasons for the decision and any due diligence performed.

### Beneficiary Communications
Record significant conversations with beneficiaries, including requests, complaints, questions, or disputes. Having a written record of these interactions prevents misunderstandings and protects both the trustee and the beneficiary.

### General Trust Decisions
A flexible template for any other decision that doesn't fit neatly into the above categories — property management, insurance, tax elections, or anything else that requires documentation.

## How to Use Guided Minutes

1. **Choose your meeting type.** Select the template that matches the decision you're documenting. TrustOffice will present the appropriate prompts.

2. **Answer the prompts.** Each template asks for specific information relevant to that type of decision. Answer thoughtfully — these prompts are designed to capture exactly what a court or beneficiary would want to see.

3. **Review your entry.** Before saving, review what you've written. Make sure your reasoning is clear and complete. You can edit at this point.

4. **Save and attach supporting documents.** If you have relevant statements, reports, emails, or other documents, attach them to the minutes. This creates a complete record in one place.

5. **TrustOffice updates your Defensibility Score.** Each completed set of minutes improves your score and ensures your administration record stays strong.

## Tips for Effective Minutes

- **Write them right away.** Don't let days pass between a decision and its documentation. Details fade quickly.
- **Be honest.** Record what actually happened and what you actually considered, not what you wish you had considered.
- **Include alternatives.** Noting what other options you considered — and why you chose differently — shows thoughtful decision-making.
- **Note who was involved.** If you consulted with a CPA, attorney, or investment advisor, say so. Professional advice strengthens your position.
- **Don't skip the small stuff.** Even routine decisions deserve documentation. Over time, a pattern of consistent record-keeping is more valuable than a few detailed entries surrounded by gaps.

Guided Minutes makes it easy to do the right thing. Use it for every decision, big or small, and you'll build a robust, defensible record of your trust administration.
