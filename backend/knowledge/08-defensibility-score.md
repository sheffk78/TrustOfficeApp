# TrustOffice Defensibility Score

## What Is the Defensibility Score?

The Defensibility Score is TrustOffice's proprietary measure of how well-documented and defensible your trust administration is. It evaluates whether you're creating the kind of paper trail that would protect you if your decisions were ever questioned — by a beneficiary, a court, or any other interested party.

Think of it as a health check for your trust management. A high score means you're documenting decisions, keeping records, and following best practices. A low score means there are gaps that could leave you vulnerable.

The score isn't about whether your decisions are right or wrong — it's about whether you can *prove* you made them carefully and in good faith.

## What the Score Measures

The Defensibility Score is built from several key components, each reflecting a different aspect of good trust administration:

### Decision Documentation

Are you recording your decisions? This component looks at whether you have documented minutes, distribution memos, and investment rationale. A decision that isn't written down is a decision that's hard to defend.

### Timeliness

Are you staying current? This measures whether you're meeting deadlines for filings, accountings, distributions, and other time-sensitive tasks. Being late suggests negligence, even if the substance of your decisions is sound.

### Asset Tracking

Do you have a complete and up-to-date Schedule A? This component checks whether your trust assets are logged, valued, and regularly updated. You can't manage what you can't see, and you can't account for assets you haven't documented.

### Beneficiary Communication

Are you keeping beneficiaries informed? This measures whether you're providing required notices, accountings, and responses to beneficiary inquiries. Good communication reduces disputes and shows you're fulfilling your duty to account.

### Compliance

Are you meeting your legal obligations? This component tracks required filings, notice deadlines, and other regulatory requirements specific to your trust and jurisdiction.

## How the Score Is Calculated

Each component is evaluated based on the activity and records within your TrustOffice account. The score is calculated as a weighted combination of these components, producing a number that reflects your overall administration defensibility.

The specific weights reflect the relative importance of each area in trust law and best practices. For example, decision documentation typically carries significant weight because it's the single most important factor in defending trustee actions.

## Why the Score Changes

Your Defensibility Score isn't static. It changes over time based on:

- **New activity:** Documenting a decision or completing a filing raises your score
- **Time passing:** Tasks that are due but not completed will lower your score
- **Gaps in documentation:** Missing records for decisions you've made reduce your score
- **Changes in requirements:** If new compliance obligations arise, your score adjusts accordingly

This means your score can go down even if you haven't done anything wrong — it may simply reflect that a periodic task is now overdue. The score is designed to prompt action, not to assign blame.

## What Each Score Range Means

### High (80-100)
Your trust administration is well-documented and current. You're meeting your obligations, keeping good records, and communicating with beneficiaries. If challenged, you'd be in a strong position to defend your actions.

### Moderate (50-79)
You're on the right track but have some gaps. Maybe you've made decisions without fully documenting them, or a filing is coming due. These are fixable — the score shows you where to focus.

### Low (Below 50)
Significant gaps exist. You may be missing key documentation, overdue on important tasks, or not communicating adequately with beneficiaries. A low score doesn't mean you've done something wrong, but it does mean you're at risk if your administration is ever questioned. Take action on the highlighted items.

## How to Improve Your Score

- **Document every decision.** Use TrustOffice's Guided Minutes to capture your reasoning, even for routine choices.
- **Stay current on filings.** Set up reminders for tax returns, accountings, and notices.
- **Keep Schedule A updated.** Log new assets, remove sold ones, and update valuations regularly.
- **Communicate with beneficiaries.** Send accountings and respond to inquiries promptly.
- **Review your score regularly.** The score tells you what needs attention — use it as your to-do list.

The Defensibility Score isn't a judgment. It's a tool to help you be the best trustee you can be.
